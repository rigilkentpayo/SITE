PASTRIES

HOPIA

Hopia is a popular Filipino pastry filled with bean
paste which was introduced by the Fujian Chinese
during the American occupation.

POLVORON

A polvorón is a type of heavy, soft, and very crumbly
Spanish shortbread made of flour, sugar, milk, and nuts.

LECHE FLAN

Filipino leche flan is a rich, steamed custard topped with a syrupy caramel.

PASTILLAS

Pastillas, literally known as milk candies,
is an integral part of food culture,
and one of the favorite sweet delicacies or desserts among Filipinos.

PUTO

Puto is a Filipino steamed rice cake, traditionally
made from slightly fermented rice dough (galapong).

CASSAVA CAKE

Cassava Cake is a classic Filipino dessert made from grated cassava (manioc).
Cassava is also known as kamoteng kahoy and balinghoy in the Philippines.

BIKO

Biko is a rich, chewy Filipino rice cake made
with sticky rice, coconut milk, and dark sugar.

UBE HALAYA

Ube halaya or halayang ube is a Philippine dessert made
from boiled and mashed purple yam Ube halaya is the
main base in ube/purple yam flavored-pastries

SIOPAO

s a Philippine steamed bun with various fillings.

PANDESAL

Pandesal is a popular yeast-raised bread in the Philippines.
Individual loaves are shaped by rolling the dough into long logs
(bastón, Spanish for "stick") which are rolled in fine bread crumbs.

BIBINGKA

a Philippine cake that is made with rice flour, water,
and sometimes other ingredients (such as butter, sugar, cheese, or coconut)
and that is traditionally cooked between banana leaves.

APPLE PIE

dish made by lining a shallow container with pastry
and filling the container with a sweet or savoury mixture.
